<p><p>The regenerative cell market primarily comprises two types: allogeneic and autologous. Allogeneic cells are derived from a donor and can be used to treat multiple patients, offering scalability and convenience. In contrast, autologous cells are harvested from the patient’s own body, ensuring compatibility and reducing the risk of rejection. Both types play crucial roles in therapies for various conditions, with allogeneic products fostering broader accessibility and autologous treatments emphasizing personalized medicine. Each approach has distinct advantages in regenerative medicine.</p></p>
