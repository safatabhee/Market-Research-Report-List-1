<p><p>Municipal plastic waste management encompasses three primary market types: landfill, recycling, and incineration. Landfill involves burying waste, often leading to environmental concerns such as land usage and pollution. Recycling focuses on processing used plastics to create new materials, promoting sustainability and resource conservation. Incineration entails burning plastic waste to generate energy, reducing volume but raising emissions and air quality issues. Each method presents distinct advantages and disadvantages, influencing municipal decisions for effective plastic waste management.</p></p>
